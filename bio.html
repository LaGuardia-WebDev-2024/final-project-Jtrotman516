Biography

Born in 1918, Leonard Bernstein was an American composer and conductor known for his passion, versatility, and musical brilliance. He rose to fame as the first American-born music director of the New York Philharmonic.

Bernstein was also a prolific composer for Broadway, film, and the concert hall. He studied at Harvard and the Curtis Institute of Music, and became a cultural icon through his televised Young People's Concerts.
